Live and Let Die (1973)

Plot

When Bond investigates the murders of three fellow agents, he finds himself a target, evading vicious assassins as he closes in on powerful Kananga. Known on the streets as Mr. Big, Kananga is coordinating a global threat, using tons of self-produced heroin. As Bond tries to unravel the mastermind's plan, he meets Solitaire, a beautiful tarot-card reader, whose magic is crucial to the crime lord.

Rating

66% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.8/10 IMDb

Opinion

7/10   Decently Good

With Connery gone and Roger Moore taking over, his first impression as James Bond is honestly not too bad. The villians are actually quite good and the overall plot is satisfactory at best. It is not really as great as Connery's first three films, which felt more like Bond is a spy and not a charismatic lover.

The Man with the Golden Gun (1974)

Plot

Cool government operative James Bond searches for a stolen invention that can turn the sun's heat into a destructive weapon. He soon crosses paths with the menacing Francisco Scaramanga, a hit man so skilled he has a seven-figure working fee. Bond then joins forces with the swimsuit-clad Mary Goodnight, and together they track Scaramanga to a tropical isle hideout where the killer-for-hire lures the slick spy into a deadly maze for a final duel.

Rating

44% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.8/10 IMDb

Opinion

7/10   Decently Good

I honestly never understood the low rating for this movie. Sure, there are some pretty bad scenes (looking at you, corkscrew jump). The villian's side character, Nick Nack, is honestly one of the best side characters I have ever seen. The duel between the villian and Bond is seriously engaging and intense. The villian almost defeats James Bond, similar to Red Grant from Sean Connery's From Russia With Love.

The Spy Who Loved Me (1977)

Plot

In a globe-trotting assignment that has him skiing off the edges of cliffs and driving a car deep underwater, British super-spy James Bond unites with sexy Russian agent Anya Amasova to defeat megalomaniac shipping magnate Karl Stromberg, who is threatening to destroy New York City with nuclear weapons. Bond's most deadly adversary on the case is Stromberg's henchman, Jaws, a seven-foot giant with terrifying steel teeth.

Rating

79% Rotten Tomotatoes

7.1/10 IMDb

Opinion

8/10   Pretty Good

This is truly the best Bond film by Roger Moore. The introduction of the iconic character, Jaws, and the female Russian spy are seriously great. However, my only issue is that the main villian, Stromberg, is not really interesting and dies pathetically easily. Aside from that, the plot is fascinating and I wish there is another James Bond film with a female spy collaborating with Bond.

Moonraker (1979)

Plot

Agent 007 blasts into orbit in this action-packed adventure that takes him to Venice, Rio De Janeiro and outer space. When Bond investigates the hijacking of an American space shuttle, he and beautiful CIA agent Holly Goodhead are soon locked in a life-or-death struggle against Hugo Drax, a power-mad industrialist whose horrific scheme may destroy all human life on earth!

Rating

61% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.3/10 IMDb

Opinion

4/10   Bad

I am surprised the ratings for this film is much higher. If you have ever watched this movie, you will see why I think it is the worst Roger Moore film ever made. The space battle is hilariously unrealistic and removes the element of being a spy. The reason why the space battle even exists is because Star Wars was popular during the time this film was in production. Jaws is also featured in this film, but went from an evil henchman to an empathetic and caring man who sides with Bond at the end of the movie. I believe the shift in Jaws's story could have been better and that he still should have despised Bond. I will give credit to the producers for making a plot about James Bond being in space, but the execution is hilariously awful.

For Your Eyes Only (1981)

Plot

British agent James Bond is dispatched to recover a strategic communications device before it finds its way into the hands of the Russians. When the secret device that controls Britain's Polaris submarines goes missing after the spy ship carrying it sinks, 007 joins forces with a vengeful woman and a wealthy hero of the Greek resistance movement to find the equipment.

Rating

71% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.8/10 IMDb

Opinion

6/10   Average

The plot twist in this film is well done and unexpected. While it's not the craziest plot twist in the world, it does drive the plot forward in an interesting way. Overall, it's not an amazing film, but it is good in its own way. There are several scenes that is boring, so that is why it lost a point.

A View to a Kill (1985)

Plot

After recovering a microchip from the body of a deceased colleague in Russia, British secret agent James Bond discovers that the technology has the potential for sinister applications. Investigating further, Bond is led to Max Zorin, the head of Zorin Industries. Soon Agent 007 faces off against the villainous Zorin and his tough Amazonian bodyguard, May Day, who are scheming to cause massive destruction that will eliminate the competition.

Rating

36% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.4/10 IMDb

Opinion

6/10   Average

This is not a bad film by all means. The problem with this movie is that Moore feels too old for this. Aside from that, the villian, Zorin, is quite insane and his plan to cause a massive tectonic plate rupture in California is a good, evil plan. Final thoughts, Roger Moore is really not bad as James Bond, but he does portray Bond very differently from other actors, who are more cold blooded and darker. Moore's take on Bond is really unique, but the fact that he went for 7 Bond movies did get boring and he should have ended either after For Your Eyes Only or Octopussy.
