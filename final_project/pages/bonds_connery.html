Dr. No (1962)

Plot

James Bond is sent to Jamaica to investigate the disappearance of a fellow British agent. The trail leads him to the underground base of Dr. No, who is plotting to disrupt an early American space launch from Cape Canaveral with a radio beam weapon.

Rating

95% Rotten Tomotatoes

7.3/10 IMDb

Opinion

8/10   Pretty Good

It is a great starting James Bond movie of the whole franchise. The introduction of James Bond is extremely iconic and well done. However, compared to its successors, it does fall behind in terms of quality and plot strength.

From Russia With Love (1963)

Plot

Agent 007 is up against the secret crime organization known as SPECTRE. Russians Rosa Klebb and Kronsteen are out to snatch a decoding device known as the Lektor, using the ravishing Tatiana to lure Bond into helping them. Bond willingly travels to meet Tatiana in Istanbul, where he must rely on his wits to escape with his life in a series of deadly encounters with the enemy.

Rating

95% Rotten Tomotatoes

7.4/10 IMDb

Opinion

9/10   Really Good

As the 2nd installment in the franchise, it does extraordinary well introducing SPECTRE, a criminal organization that shows up in the next several movies. Bond's rival, Red Grant, is easily one of the top best rivals Bond had to challenge due to expert hand to hand combat skills and assassinating. Easily one of the top five best James Bond movies ever.

Goldfinger (1964)

Plot

The powerful tycoon Auric Goldfinger has initiated Operation Grand Slam, a cataclysmic scheme to raid Fort Knox and obliterate the world economy. James Bond, armed with his specially equipped Aston Martin, must stop the plan by overcoming several outrageous adversaries.

Rating

98% Rotten Tomotatoes

7.7/10 IMDb

Opinion

10/10   Extremely Good

Coming in as the 3rd movie of Sean Connery's era, it is considered by many to be the best James Bond film ever produced. The plot is extremely engaging and Goldfinger, the villian, has an absurd and sinister plan to jack up the price of gold by destroying the United State's gold at Fort Knox.

Thunderball (1965)

Plot

Led by one-eyed evil mastermind Emilio Largo, the terrorist group SPECTRE hijacks two warheads from a NATO plane and threatens widespread nuclear destruction to extort 100 million pounds. The dashing Agent 007, James Bond, is sent to recover the warheads from the heart of Largo's lair in the Bahamas, facing underwater attacks from sharks and men alike. He must also convince the enchanting Domino, Largo's mistress, to become a key ally.

Rating

88% Rotten Tomotatoes

7/10 IMDb

Opinion

7/10   Decently Good

This film is still enjoyable to watch, but the absurd amount of film time spent under water is too high. The under water scenes are very slow and it is sometimes very hard to actually understand what is happening when fight scenes starts happening. But besides that, the plot is very interesting and Largo, the villian, is actually really cool.

You Only Live Twice (1967)

Plot

During the Cold War, American and Russian spacecrafts go missing, leaving each superpower believing the other is to blame. As the world teeters on the brink of nuclear war, British intelligence learns that one of the crafts has landed in the Sea of Japan. After faking his own death, secret agent James Bond is sent to investigate. In Japan, he's aided by Tiger Tanaka and the beautiful Aki, who help him uncover a sinister global conspiracy.

Rating

73% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.9/10 IMDb

Opinion

5/10   Okay

Most people would rate this higher, but I find this movie to be incredibly boring up until the ending. The introduction of Ernst Stavro Blofeld who is the leader of SPECTRE is really nice, but felt wasted and not really exciting at all. When he was revealed, he just appears and looks at James Bond then resumes with his important mission. The buildup of the plot is average, and the final action scene was really the only one that was amazing to watch.

Diamonds Are Forever (1971)

Plot

James Bond, equipped with an armoury of hi-tech gadgets, infiltrates a Las Vegas diamond-smuggling ring in a bid to foil a plot to target Washington with a laser in space. However, as 007 prepares to tackle the evil Blofeld, the mastermind who threatens to destabilise the world, he is captivated by the delicious Tiffany Case - but is she really a double agent?

Rating

66% Rotten Tomotatoes

6.6/10 IMDb

Opinion

4/10   Bad

Originally, Sean Conner was meant to retire as James Bond after You Only Live Twice but was given a chance to do another film. The villian is the same villian as the last Connery film, Blofeld, but different actor. Blofeld in this movie is significantly worse than Blofeld from You Only Live Twice mostly due to the fact that Blofeld in this one is silly and not as sinister as the previous. This film could have been a lot better if Connery did not return since you can tell by his face that he is getting too old. This is not his last film as James Bond since he does do another one called Never Say Never Again which is based off of Ian Fleming's novel, Thunderball. It will not be mentioned because I do not consider it canon and it is awful.
